Your task

For each pair of documents, you will answer two independent questions:

Question 1 — Readability

Which document is more readable and intact?

Consider: Is the text well-extracted from the PDF? Is it coherent Swedish? Is it free from garbled characters, broken sentences, or extraction artifacts? A readable document flows naturally and is easy to follow.

Question 2 — Substance

Which document contains more substantive, broadly useful information?

Consider: Does the content teach something widely applicable, or is it trivial, marketing copy, or procedural filler? Substantive documents impart knowledge that could benefit a general reader.

Key principles

  • These are independent: A document can be highly readable but trivial in content, or it can be substantive but poorly extracted. Judge each question separately.
  • You see excerpts: Documents are shown as the first 3,000 characters of longer PDFs. Judge only what is in front of you.
  • Force a choice: Use the 4-point scale even when the choice is close. Use "slightly" when you are unsure — do not skip just because it is hard.
  • Skip sparingly: Use "Skip" ONLY when a document is genuinely unrateable due to extraction failure, wrong language, or mistranslation — NOT simply because the choice is difficult.
  • Timing is recorded: Do not rush; accuracy matters more than speed.

Study structure

This study has 3 sessions of 15 pairs each, approximately 30 minutes per session. Please take a 10-minute break between sessions. You can complete sessions on different days if needed.
